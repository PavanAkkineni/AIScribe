# Test Conversation Example
# You can use this as a reference for what the system expects

doctor: Good morning! How are you doing today?

patient: Good morning, doctor. I'm not feeling well. I've had a high fever for the past three days.

doctor: I see. Can you tell me more about your symptoms? What's your temperature been like?

patient: It's been around 102 to 103 degrees Fahrenheit. I also have some body aches and a slight runny nose.

doctor: Any other symptoms like cough, sore throat, or difficulty breathing?

patient: Just a mild cough occasionally, nothing too severe.

doctor: Okay. Have you taken any medication for the fever?

patient: Yes, I've been taking Tylenol, but it only helps temporarily.

doctor: I understand. Based on your symptoms, it appears you have a severe fever with mild cold symptoms. Let me examine you quickly. *pauses* Your vitals look okay aside from the elevated temperature. This could be a viral infection, but I want to ensure we manage this properly.

patient: What should I do, doctor?

doctor: I'm going to prescribe you some medications. First, Doro 150 for the fever - take it twice daily. Then Cyprosine for any allergic reactions or to help with the cold symptoms. I'll also prescribe Indolo-160 for inflammation and pain, and Citrogen for immune support.

patient: Okay, thank you doctor.

doctor: However, given the severity of your fever, I'd recommend you also see another physician for a more comprehensive evaluation and possibly some blood work to rule out any bacterial infections.

patient: Should I come back if it doesn't get better?

doctor: Yes, definitely. If your fever persists beyond three more days or gets worse, please return immediately. For now, these medications should help manage your symptoms. Make sure to rest well and stay hydrated.

patient: Thank you so much, doctor. I'll follow your advice.

doctor: You're welcome. Take care and feel better soon!
